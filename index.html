About

I'm a protocol engineer and DeFi founder with 5+ years of deep experience shipping production-grade blockchain infrastructure. My career spans smart contracts, protocol design, advanced mathematics, backend systems, and cross-chain infrastructure.

Before diving fully into web3, I spent 3 years at MediaTek as a hardware engineer working on chip design and validation. That background instilled rigorous system thinking — designing for edge cases, understanding constraints, and optimizing for production at scale.

I've co-founded and shipped two DeFi protocols from zero to production: a decentralized perpetual futures exchange with a decentralized limit order book on a custom Avalanche Subnet (evolved from a Curve-style vAMM in V1), and a leveraged lending protocol on Aptos that peaked at $7M TVL. I own smart contracts end-to-end and contribute significantly to backend systems.

My technical depth includes order book systems, AMM mathematics (Curve StableSwap, CurveCrypto invariants), liquidation engines, and cross-chain interoperability. I'm comfortable with complex numerical methods like Newton's method for on-chain computation.

Currently exploring the intersection of AI and finance — particularly the agent economy, where autonomous AI agents transact, manage assets, and operate as independent economic actors. I'm interested in building the financial infrastructure that treats agents as first-class users: programmable credit, inference insurance, and machine-to-machine payment rails.
